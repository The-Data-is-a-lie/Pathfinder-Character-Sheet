# Frequency-Based Layout

Design the sheet around **session behavior**, not chapter order. In Pathfinder,
players repeatedly need initiative-adjacent defenses, attack math, HP/resources,
and a short list of active options (maneuvers, spells, conditionals). Background
lore, full feat text, and long inventory notes change far less often.

## Practical ranking for Pathfinder play

**Tier 1 — every round / every check**

- Ability modifiers (not just raw scores)
- AC and key save DCs / bonuses
- Attack bonuses, damage dice, crit range
- Current HP / temporary HP / nonlethal (when tracked)
- Active conditions and stance / buff toggles that alter rolls

**Tier 2 — every encounter or scene**

- Skills most used by the build
- Spells / spheres / maneuvers ready this fight
- Consumables and limited-use abilities
- CMB / CMD and special combat options

**Tier 3 — between sessions or on demand**

- Full feat and class-feature text
- Biography, deity, traits narrative
- Complete inventory catalogs and treasure notes
- Settings and library management

## Design implications

- On multi-page or multi-tab sheets, **page one / Summary / Combat** owns Tier 1.
- Character name should be large and, on multi-page printouts, **repeated per page**.
- Leave explicit room for **current** values of volatile stats (HP, ammo, rounds),
  separate from maxima when both matter.
- Digital sheets can demote Tier 3 into tabs without deleting it — print layouts
  should still put Tier 1 on the first printed surface.
