# Information Architecture

A character sheet is the player’s **primary UI into the rules**. Pathfinder packs
more fields than most systems, so architecture choices dominate usability more
than ornamentation does. Group related mechanics, prioritize by use frequency,
and progressively disclose depth (tabs, drawers, expandable sections) instead of
flattening everything onto one wall of numbers.

## Concepts

- [frequency-based layout](/oks/pathfinder-character-sheet-design/information-architecture/frequency-based-layout.md) — put what players touch every round first.
- [grouping related stats](/oks/pathfinder-character-sheet-design/information-architecture/grouping-related-stats.md) — combat with combat, gear with encumbrance, identity at the top.
- [progressive disclosure](/oks/pathfinder-character-sheet-design/information-architecture/progressive-disclosure.md) — tabs, drawers, and expandable detail for dense systems.
- [session vs. identity data](/oks/pathfinder-character-sheet-design/information-architecture/session-vs-identity-data.md) — stable character facts vs. values that change mid-session.
